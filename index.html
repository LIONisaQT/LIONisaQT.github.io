Game Design and Development Projects

Shroommates |

Made with Unreal Engine (currently work in progress)

"Housing isn't cheap here, mate."

Shroommates is third-person stealth game where users play as a sentient mushroom trying to grow in secret in a UCSC student's apartment. The mushroom can grow by consuming food and staying out of direct sunlight. Players must adapt to different sizes, as size dynamically alters speed, hiding ability, and places you can get to. Complete objectives while trying to keep out of sight of the AI human! Click here to sneakily sneak into the repository. I won't tell anyone you were there.

  • Lead developer: Leading and delegating tasks to six other programmers.
  • AI programmer: Quickly implemented working temporary AI sufficient for prototyping purposes within a few days. Currently porting old AI functions and behaviors into newer, more robust AI, who is controled by an improved behavior tree.
  • Generalist programmer: Implementing game behaviors as needed in various areas, such as state management and game objects.
  • Idenified, troubleshooted, and later explained to some team members Git issues and their solutions.
  • Point person for communication and technical QA with professors.

MinShmup |

Written in Java using libGDX and Android Studio (currently on hiatus due to school)

"Game juice." -Tad Leckman

MinShmup is a simple, minimalistic shoot-em up for Android. The intention of the project is for me to learn in-depth about and play with various methods and functions libGDX has to offer through a simple game. I also wanted to make a simple game feel amazing to play, taking inspriration from grapefrukt games' juicy-breakout. For the player, I want to make a game that looks aesthetically pleasing, satisfying to play, and easy to pick up to kill time with. As a developer, I want to make a light-weight game with robust, easy-to-read code, as well as use the project to exercise good game development practices. MinShmup currently doesn't have a public repository on GitHub, but will in the upcoming weeks.

  • Feels great to play, even in its prototype stage – the player receives pleasing and appropriate visual and audio feedback for various in-game actions.
  • Fully takes advantage of libGDX's object pooling system to handle disposable objects, thus preventing any memory leaks and decreasing overall memory usage.
  • More libGDX methods to be implemented to optimize and juice up the game even further in the future.

Engineering Projects

Food Now! (Project Owner) |

Can't choose a place to eat? Get food now!

"Ew no I ate there a couple of days ago, choose another place." -person who no longer has friends

Food Now! is a web application that aims to solve the problem of indecisive friends choosing a place to eat by being the final say in picking restaurants. The app takes in everyone's food preferences, a distance and price maximum, and then returns a random restaurant that respects the distance and price limit while best fulfilling at least one of those preferences. Uses React for front-end design, Express for the server framework, and Google Firebase for user login. Check out the repository here, and make sure to leave a review!

  • Project owner: Led and delegated tasks to five other developers.
  • Sent user parameters from front-end to Yelp, handled list of restaurants returned by Yelp, and passed a random restaurant back to front-end to display to the user.

Gotcho Back |

HACK UCSC 2016: Tech Cares category, 1st place

"Oh no, not Gotcho Back!" -your potentional kidnapper

According to multiple studies such as this one, a good amount of women feel it is unsafe for them to walk outside at night by themselves. Enter Gotcho Back, a safety app prototype for Android. Users pick a contact and set a timer to get home. If the timer runs out, the phone will vibrate and ask the user if they need more time. If the user does not respond within a specific timeframe, the phone will send a text to the contact and ring. After further lack of response, the app will call the contact. Feel safe at the repository here. Click the Devpost link for win proof.

  • Implemented vibration trigger.
  • My first hackathon win!
